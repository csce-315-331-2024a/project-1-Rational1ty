Service

As a college student majoring in engineering, I possess a unique skill set that can be harnessed to serve my community in several ways. For me, it starts with my family—they are the ones who have given the most to me, so I try to give back to them whenever I can.

When it comes to my younger siblings, I offer educational support whenever they need it by helping with homework, introducing difficult concepts in interesting ways, and inspiring curiosity. My goal is to help them be driven to learn new things and to grow into intelligent and well-rounded people.

To serve my parents, I try to be first of all a good son, which means respecting their wisdom, taking their advice, and being a good role model for my siblings. I offer some of the money that I earn from my job to my mom so that she can pay for improvements to the home, or put extra money towards my siblings' education. I also perform small acts of service when I'm at the house, like helping out with physical labor or fixing things that are broken.

Within the college environment, I serve my classmates by trying to foster an atmosphere where people know that they can share their ideas freely. Inviting others to study sessions, sharing project insights, and providing assistance with difficult engineering problems contribute to a supportive academic community, creating an environment where everyone can thrive.

Finally, I give back to the community as a whole by engaging in outreach programs and volunteering at various local organizations, such as the Aggieland Humane Society. I also leverage my engineering expertise to address local challenges, like helping neighbors and family friends fix things. And sometimes, I serve others by just saying “yes” when they ask for a favor. By using my skills to serve others, I not only improve my own life but play an important role in advancing the well-being of the community as a whole.

Areas of Improvement

Although I belive that one of the best ways to support your community is to support yourself first, I will be the first to admit that there is much more I could do to support my community.

In the future, I want to engage more with volunteering efforts, especially in areas where my engineering skills can be of use. I'm also going to make an effort to connect with more community STEM engagement events, where I can share my passion for engineering, help get kids more invested in education, and influence the community in a positive way.

Finally, I hope that once I graduate, I'll be able to support my community by contributing my skills and career experience, as well as donate to local organizations using my newly-acquired wealth.
